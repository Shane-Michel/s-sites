Your domain needs a home. Let’s pick the right host for your website goals.

What Is Hosting?

Hosting is where your website lives — it's the server that delivers your pages when people visit your domain. It determines your site's speed, uptime, and security.

There are multiple hosting types, each with pros, cons, and price points. Here’s the breakdown:

🌐 Shared Hosting

Inexpensive and easy. Your site shares server resources with others. Good for beginners, but slower and less secure under traffic.

  • Bluehost – ~$3/mo (starter)
  • HostGator – similar pricing

⚙️ VPS Hosting

More power, more control. VPS gives you a virtual slice of a dedicated server. Requires technical setup but offers better performance and scalability.

  • A2 Hosting – ~$40/mo
  • Cloudways – scalable VPS

☁️ Cloud Hosting

Modern, scalable, and performance-optimized. Run on global networks like AWS or Google Cloud. Often pay-as-you-go, best for devs or growing sites.

  • Vercel – Free for small static sites
  • Netlify – Also free for basic plans

🧱 Static Site Hosting

Perfect for simple HTML/JS/CSS sites. Lightning fast, secure, and often free. Best for devs or landing pages.

  • GitHub Pages – Free
  • Surge.sh – Free for small sites

What Should You Look For?

  • Uptime: 99.9% or better
  • Speed: SSD storage and optimized caching
  • Ease of Use: cPanel or modern dashboard
  • Support: 24/7 chat or phone preferred
  • SSL: Free SSL certificates are standard now

Estimated Hosting Cost: $5–$25/month depending on type and provider
