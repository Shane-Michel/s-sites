Step 10: Set Up Professional Email

Build trust, look legit, and stop using Gmail for business. Here's how to create email addresses on your own domain.

Why You Need a Professional Email

Imagine two businesses: one emails you from coolbiz@gmail.com, and the other from info@coolbiz.com. Who do you trust more?
Your email address is part of your brand. If your domain is ready, it’s time to attach email to it.

📦 Email Hosting Providers

  • Google Workspace — starts at $6/month
  • Zoho Mail — free tier available
  • Namecheap — often includes 2 free mailboxes
  • iCloud Mail (Custom Domain) — free with Apple

📡 What You'll Need

  • Your registered domain (e.g., coolbiz.com)
  • Access to DNS settings (usually at your registrar)
  • Ability to create TXT, MX, and CNAME records

🛠 Setup Steps

  1. Choose an email provider
  2. Sign up and verify your domain
  3. Add MX records from your provider to your DNS
  4. Wait 30 minutes – 24 hours for propagation
  5. Create mailboxes like info@ or support@

🔐 Security Tips

  • Enable SPF, DKIM, and DMARC for anti-spam
  • Use strong passwords and 2FA
  • Don’t share inbox logins — use aliases or users

Pricing Breakdown

  • Google Workspace: $6–12/month per user
  • Zoho Mail: Free (lite), or $1–4/month for pro
  • Namecheap Email: Often free with domain for 2 months

Estimated Setup Time: 1–2 hours including DNS
