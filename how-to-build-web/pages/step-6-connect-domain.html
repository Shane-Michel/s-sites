Step 6: Connect Domain + Hosting

This is the part where you tell the world where your site lives. DNS, nameservers, and SSL — let’s go.

What You're Doing Here

You're pointing your domain (like mycoolsite.com) to your hosting provider (like Bluehost or Vercel) so the internet knows where your website files live.

🔁 Update Nameservers

If you’re using a traditional host (like Bluehost, SiteGround, etc.):

  • Login to your domain registrar (Namecheap, GoDaddy, etc.)
  • Find the DNS or Nameserver settings
  • Replace default nameservers with those from your host
  • Example: ns1.bluehost.com and ns2.bluehost.com

📍 Add A Records (for advanced setups)

If your host gave you an IP address (common for VPS or static hosts like Vercel):

  • Go to domain DNS settings
  • Add an A record pointing to your server IP
  • Also add a CNAME for www pointing to @

🔐 Add SSL (HTTPS)

Modern browsers flag non-HTTPS sites. Most hosting providers offer free SSL certificates.

  • Enable HTTPS in your hosting dashboard
  • Use Let's Encrypt if hosting manually
  • Test your site at WhyNoPadlock.com

⏱ Wait for Propagation

DNS changes can take 10 minutes — or up to 48 hours — to fully propagate.

  • Use dnschecker.org to monitor
  • Clear your browser cache when testing
  • Don’t panic — just give it time

Estimated Time

Most users can get their domain fully connected in 30–90 minutes, but full global propagation can take up to 48 hours.
